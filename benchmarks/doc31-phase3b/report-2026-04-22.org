* Interpretation

`Repair success' means `sexp-cst-repair' returned a
`repair-result' envelope (not a typed error).  For the
`unfixable' category we *expect* the tool to refuse — so
a high `expected-match' there is the right outcome, not a
high `repaired' rate.

`Retry-rate reduction' models the ROI thesis: without the
repair tool, every broken case would have forced the LLM
to retry; with the tool, only the residual unfixable cases
need retry.  That gap is the token / latency saving Phase
3a buys the pipeline.
